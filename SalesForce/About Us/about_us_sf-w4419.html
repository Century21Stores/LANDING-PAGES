We Pioneered Off-Price

In 1961, co-founders Al and Sonny Gindi opened the first off-price retailer in Downtown Manhattan. Inspired by an upcoming World’s Fair exhibit “Century 21, The World of Tomorrow” the name represents the owners’ mission to revamp the traditional retail model, allowing shoppers to access designer brands at amazing prices.

What started as a small, discount department store has evolved into a must-see shopping destination for locals, tourists and celebrities alike. Now, the flagship store spans six buildings, up from a mere three floors from when it first opened, and there are 14 locations spanning the east coast.

Everyone Has A Century Story

We all live for the moments we find It—the piece we want to show and tell everyone about. For Richard Martin, curator of the Costume Institute, it was a John Galliano dress. ”Absolutely off the rack,” he said. “In fact, falling off the rack.” The dress would later be showcased in the Met show “Our New Clothes: Acquisitions of the 1990’s.”

Century Does Fashion Week

Our flagship has been front row for a lineup of NYFW events. Telfar, the menswear line of fashion prodigy and international celebrity Telfar Clemens, showcased their Fall/Winter collection, allowing the public to get a sneak peek the same time as the insiders.
